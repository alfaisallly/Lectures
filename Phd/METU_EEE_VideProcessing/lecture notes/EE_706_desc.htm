The purpose of this course is to present a comprehensive description of digital video processing. The course will start with characteristics of basic analog video systems. Digital video concepts will then be presented. Topics will include filtering, enhancement and motion estimation. Finally a comprehensive description of digital video compression techniques such as H.26x and MPEG-x; archival and transmission of digital video information will be presented.

Course Outline

·    Basics of analog and digital video: color video formation and specification, analog TV system, video raster, digital video formats, spatial and temporal frequency response of the human visual system.  (1 week)

·    2D motion estimation: Motion modeling, optical flow equation, different motion estimation methods (pel-based, block-based, mesh-based, global motion estimation, multi-resolution approach)  (1 week)

·    Video Image Filtering: Motion compensated filtering, enhancement, deinterlacing, standards conversion, superresolution  (1 week)

·    Basic compression techniques: information bounds for lossless and lossy source coding, binary encoding, scalar/vector quantization (1 week)

·    Waveform-based coding: transform coding, predictive coding, motion compensated coding, scalable video coding  (1 week)

·    Object-Based Coding : 2D model based coding, 3D model based coding (1 week)

·    Video Compression Standards and Applications: H.261, H.263, H.264, MPEG-1/2/4 (1 week)

·    Content-Based Video Indexing and MPEG-7: feature extraction, video segmentation, representation (1 week)

·    Digital Video Communication: video streaming, error control in video communications (1 week)
